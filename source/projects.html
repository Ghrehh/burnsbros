Internal Projects

Experimentation and continuous learning are huge parts of what we’re all about here at Burns Bros. & Co. So much so that we schedule regular studio time to focus on our own side projects.

We use this time to reflect on how we run our studio. We dig deep and look hard at things like: how we position our business; how we communicate & collaborate both internally (pretty integral stuff considering we’re a remote studio) and externally with our clients; and the processes and methods that we use to organise and produce work.

Our experiments challenge us to validate how we: operate, attract new business, generate ideas, conceptualise, plan, design and ultimately build digital products.

By sandboxing time, we give ourselves space in which to broaden our horizons, look at problems in new ways and keep up-to-date with the latest advancements in design and tech. We get great satisfaction from bringing what we’ve learned from our experiments into our day-to-day client work.

We enjoy getting out of our beds and beginning work each morning. We’re aiming to keep things that way through practicing continuous innovation, experimentation, studio hacking and learning. Combined, all of this keeps us at the top of our game and working in a sustainable way on interesting things. We want to continue creating work for great people that solves big problems.

To date our in-house experiments have resulted in the creation of tools that can do things like: teach music students to read sheet music and train their ears to recognise intervals, and apps that scour social media platforms for emerging patterns. Meanwhile, our studio hacking workshops have helped us tweak and strengthen our operational models, core values, strategies, workflows, production processes, etc.

We’re aiming to share a lot more about our side projects, experiments, workshops and organisational set-up. Look out for further updates on our blog!
